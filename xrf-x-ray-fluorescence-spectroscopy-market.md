<p><p>X-ray fluorescence (XRF) spectroscopy is a non-destructive analytical technique used to determine the elemental composition of materials. Utilizing X-rays to excite atoms in a sample, XRF detects the characteristic secondary X-rays emitted, providing detailed information about the elements present. This method is widely applied across various industries, including environmental monitoring, material science, and metallurgy, due to its accuracy and ability to analyze solid, liquid, and powder samples.</p><p>The XRF market is experiencing significant growth driven by technological advancements, increasing demand for metal and mineral analysis, and stringent regulations concerning environmental monitoring. The rising focus on sustainability and recycling also boosts the adoption of XRF in waste management and resource recovery sectors.
