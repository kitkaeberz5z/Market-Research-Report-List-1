<p><p>The biodegradable plastic packaging market encompasses several key types, including starch-based plastics, PLA (polylactic acid), PBS (polybutylene succinate), PBAT (polybutylene adipate terephthalate), and PHA (polyhydroxyalkanoates). Starch-based plastics derive from renewable resources, offering compostability. PLA, produced from cornstarch, is widely used for its clarity and strength. PBS and PBAT are petroleum-based but are compostable, while PHA is produced by microbial fermentation and is highly degradable. Other materials may include various blends or emerging bioplastic technologies aimed at reducing environmental impact.</p></p>
